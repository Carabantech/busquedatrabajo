# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

This file accumulates your best interview stories over time. Each evaluation (Block F) adds new stories here. Instead of memorizing 100 answers, maintain 5-10 deep stories that you can bend to answer almost any behavioral question.

## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

### [Delivery] Regional IT Leadership Across LATAM
**Source:** Report #001 - AgileEngine - Delivery Manager ID51393
**S (Situation):** In CME-Wakapi, operations and infrastructure had to support multiple countries with coordination across LATAM, the United States, and Lebanon.
**T (Task):** Keep operations reliable while aligning technology planning, purchases, vendors, and executive reporting.
**A (Action):** Led regional coordination, managed vendors and purchases, aligned stakeholders, and maintained executive visibility through KPI reporting.
**R (Result):** Sustained regional IT operations and enabled technology expansion with clearer decision-making and operational control.
**Reflection:** Senior leadership is not just solving technical problems; it is creating enough structure and communication that other teams can move faster with less friction.
**Best for questions about:** leadership, stakeholder management, regional coordination, operations, executive communication

### [Project Delivery] Autódromo Villicum High-Availability Infrastructure
**Source:** Report #001 - AgileEngine - Delivery Manager ID51393
**S (Situation):** A technology rollout for the Autódromo Villicum required robust infrastructure, networking, cameras, and high-availability interconnections.
**T (Task):** Deliver the project end-to-end with the right technical design and purchasing decisions.
**A (Action):** Directed the work, defined the network and infrastructure approach, handled purchasing, and coordinated implementation.
**R (Result):** The project was deployed and put into operation with the required connectivity and infrastructure base.
**Reflection:** The biggest delivery risk in infrastructure projects is usually misalignment between design, procurement, and field execution; integrating those early prevents rework later.
**Best for questions about:** end-to-end delivery, project ownership, risk management, execution under constraints

### [Cross-functional Delivery] EDUC.AR Connectivity Program
**Source:** Report #001 - AgileEngine - Delivery Manager ID51393
**S (Situation):** The EDUC.AR initiative required interconnection of educational institutions with fiber infrastructure across Mendoza and Paraná.
**T (Task):** Design the network and coordinate the team to deliver the rollout.
**A (Action):** Led planning, network design, and team coordination across the implementation effort.
**R (Result):** The project advanced with a structured delivery model and aligned technical execution.
**Reflection:** Multi-site delivery depends less on heroics and more on repeatable coordination, clear priorities, and visibility across moving parts.
**Best for questions about:** delivery management, team coordination, planning, infrastructure programs, cross-site execution
